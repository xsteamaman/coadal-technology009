Data modeling

Data modeling is the process of creating a conceptual representation of data structures and relationships to support the storage, retrieval, and manipulation of data within a database or information system. It involves identifying entities, attributes, and relationships, and then organizing them into a logical schema that reflects the real-world domain being modeled. This facilitates data storage, querying, and analysis, providing a framework for understanding and managing data effectively.

Data Testing

Data testing is the process of verifying the accuracy, completeness, and reliability of data within a system or database. It involves performing various tests and validations to ensure that the data meets quality standards and is fit for its intended purpose. This includes checking for consistency, correctness, conformity to business rules, and adherence to data integrity constraints. Data testing helps identify errors, anomalies, or discrepancies in the data, enabling organizations to make informed decisions and maintain data integrity.

Data warehouese platforms

Data warehouse platforms are software systems designed to store and manage large volumes of structured and unstructured data from various sources. Popular examples include Amazon Redshift, Google BigQuery, and Snowflake, offering features for data integration, storage, querying, and analysis. These platforms enable organizations to consolidate data from disparate sources, derive insights, and make informed decisions for business purposes.
